I co-developed, with the local team, a tool to manage ambulance dispatches at the Red Cross base in Amares, Portugal. The interface was designed for paramedics to log incidents quickly and with fewer errors. The system includes crew management, ambulance availability, and access levels. All records are stored digitally, making it easy to retrieve the full history of dispatches and responsible staff.

<img
  class="my-6 rounded-md shadow-md"
  src="/projects/cvalog/screenshot1.webp"
/>

The registration flow is split into steps to speed up completion and link each incident to the available ambulance, maintaining real-time operational control.

  class="mt-6 mb-8 rounded-md shadow-md"
  src="/projects/cvalog/screenshot2.webp"

<h3 class="font-title mb-4 text-lg font-medium">June updates</h3>

I built the core registry, translating the business rules into code: edits allowed only for a limited time and visibility restricted to the record authors. On the dashboard, recent entries are highlighted and editable; in the records section, date filters support fast lookup.
